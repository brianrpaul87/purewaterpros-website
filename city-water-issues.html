City Water Issues

Municipal water is treated to be safe, but homeowners often want better taste, fewer byproducts, and protection from older plumbing. Below are common city‑water issues and solutions.

Chlorine / Chloramine Taste & Odour

Symptoms: pool‑like taste/smell, dry skin/hair.

Solutions: Whole‑home catalytic carbon to dechlorinate showers/laundry; under‑sink carbon/RO for drinking water. For chloramine, use catalytic carbon.

Hardness / Scale

Symptoms: spots on fixtures/dishes, scale on elements, film on glass.

Solutions: Water softener or scale‑control; RO at the tap for spot‑free kettles/coffee.

Lead & Copper (Older Plumbing / Service Lines)

Risk: lead can leach from old lines or fixtures; copper from corrosion.

Solutions: Certified lead‑reduction filters at the tap; whole‑home corrosion control where applicable; RO at the kitchen tap; replace old fixtures/sections when possible.

Disinfection By‑Products (THMs/HAAs)

Formed when chlorine reacts with organics; varies by season and source.

Solutions: Catalytic carbon (whole‑home or under‑sink) and RO for drinking water. Replace filters on schedule.

PFAS (“Forever Chemicals”)

Emerging contaminants of concern in many municipalities.

Solutions: RO at the tap and specific carbon blocks rated for PFAS reduction; follow filter change schedules.

Taste / Odour / Colour (Non‑chlorine)

Symptoms: earthy/musty tastes, seasonal odours, occasional colour.

Solutions: Carbon filtration, sediment pre‑filtering if needed, and RO for drinking water.
