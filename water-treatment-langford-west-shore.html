Water sources and property types

Municipal water, private wells and existing equipment.

The West Shore includes dense municipal neighbourhoods, rural properties and private-well systems. Treatment should be selected around the actual source, water chemistry, pressure, flow, household demand and equipment already installed.

Whole-home filtration

Carbon, sediment and cartridge filtration for chlorine, taste, odour and equipment protection.

Water softeners

Hardness testing, sizing, installation, programming, repair and maintenance.

Private-well treatment

Iron, sulfur, manganese, UV disinfection, oxidation and chemical-feed systems.

Low-yield wells

Reservoir, cistern and holding-tank planning for slow-recovery wells and peak demand.

Reverse osmosis

Under-sink drinking-water treatment, filter changes, membranes and sanitizing.

Service and repairs

Troubleshooting for softeners, filters, UV, RO and chemical-feed equipment.

How appointments work

A regional service process built around the actual job.

Travel and scheduling are planned around the service type so the technician arrives prepared with the right testing and likely parts.

  1. 1
    Describe the property and problem

    Share the community, water source, symptoms and equipment already installed.

  2. 2
    Prepare the assessment

    Identify likely tests, photos, manuals or parts that may be useful before the visit.

  3. 3
    Test and inspect on site

    Confirm the water condition, flow, plumbing and equipment behaviour.

  4. 4
    Explain the options

    Review practical treatment or service choices, limitations and maintenance expectations.
