Private-well treatment

Iron and sulfur treatment designed around the water.

Orange staining, metallic water and rotten-egg odours can come from different conditions. Treatment depends on the chemistry, where the symptom appears and how much flow the home needs for homes in Victoria, Langford and surrounding Greater Victoria communities.

What the assessment considers

The assessment should separate

  • Ferrous and oxidized iron
  • Manganese and dark staining
  • Hydrogen sulfide and hot-water odours
  • pH, alkalinity and oxidation demand
  • Existing filters, softeners and plumbing

Practical system planning

Not every iron or odour problem needs the same filter.

Air oxidation, catalytic media, softening, chemical feed, contact tanks and other approaches can all have a place. The correct sequence depends on the contaminant level, pH, flow, maintenance expectations and whether the issue is in the raw water or only the hot-water system.

Iron staining

Identify the form and concentration of iron before selecting oxidation, filtration or softening.

Manganese

Account for dark staining and the pH and oxidation conditions needed for effective removal.

Sulfur odour

Determine whether the odour is in hot water, cold water or both before treating the whole home.

Oxidation and filtration

Use air, catalytic media or chemical oxidation when the water and application support it.

Chemical-feed options

Consider chlorine or peroxide injection with appropriate contact and filtration where warranted.

Maintenance planning

Explain backwashing, media care, chemical replenishment, cleaning and expected service intervals.

How it works

Treat the cause, then verify the result.

Equipment is selected only after the water, household demand, existing plumbing and maintenance expectations are understood.

  1. 1
    Test the raw water

    Review iron, manganese, pH, hardness, sulfur indicators and any available laboratory results.

  2. 2
    Locate the symptom

    Compare hot and cold water and inspect existing treatment and plumbing conditions.

  3. 3
    Select the treatment train

    Choose oxidation, filtration, softening or chemical feed in the correct order.

  4. 4
    Verify and maintain

    Confirm treated-water results and establish cleaning, chemical and backwash requirements.
