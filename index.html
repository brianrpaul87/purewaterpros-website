How water treatment should work

Test first. Explain clearly. Recommend only what fits.

The goal is to understand the water source, symptoms, plumbing, existing equipment and desired result before recommending a system.

Technology-supported assessment. Technician-led recommendations.

An in-house decision-support system organizes test results, equipment details, flow requirements and site conditions. It supports—but never replaces—on-site testing and technician judgment.

  1. 1
    Describe the problem

    Start with the symptom, water source and any equipment already installed.

  2. 2
    Test and inspect

    Review relevant water readings, flow, plumbing and site conditions.

  3. 3
    Compare sensible options

    Understand what each option solves, what it does not solve and the expected maintenance.

  4. 4
    Install and verify

    Confirm performance after installation and leave the system understandable to the homeowner.

Water treatment questions

Frequently asked questions.

Clear preliminary answers for Greater Victoria homeowners planning a water assessment, repair or treatment upgrade.

What water treatment services will Pure Water Pros offer?

Planned opening services include water softeners, reverse osmosis drinking-water systems, UV disinfection, iron and sulfur treatment, cartridge filtration, equipment repair, filter changes and scheduled maintenance.

Will you work with both municipal water and private wells?

Yes. The assessment process is different for municipal and private-well water, so recommendations will be based on the water source, symptoms, test results, flow requirements and equipment already installed.

Which areas will Pure Water Pros serve?

Pure Water Pros is preparing to provide scheduled service throughout Victoria, Langford, Colwood, View Royal, Saanich, Esquimalt, Oak Bay, Highlands, Metchosin, Sooke, Central Saanich, North Saanich and Sidney. Availability may vary by service type, travel requirements and scheduling capacity.

Can I request an appointment before opening?

Yes. The pre-launch form reserves a place for follow-up when scheduling opens. It is not a confirmed appointment and does not promise immediate service.

Do you recommend equipment before testing the water?

No. Pure Water Pros is being built around a test-first approach. The water, plumbing, flow and existing system should be understood before equipment is recommended.

How do you decide which treatment system is appropriate?

Recommendations are based on water testing, flow requirements, household needs, existing equipment and site conditions. Pure Water Pros also uses an in-house decision-support system to organize those findings and compare suitable treatment approaches. Final recommendations are reviewed and explained by the technician.
