Ultraviolet disinfection

UV water treatment and maintenance.

UV can provide continuous disinfection without adding a chemical residual, but only when the water is clear enough, the flow is controlled and the system is maintained for homes in Victoria, Langford and surrounding Greater Victoria communities.

What the assessment considers

UV systems require

  • Appropriate microbiological testing
  • Suitable water clarity and pretreatment
  • Correct peak-flow sizing
  • Annual lamp replacement
  • Clean quartz sleeves and working alarms

Practical system planning

The UV chamber is only one part of the protection plan.

Sediment, hardness, iron, manganese and colour can reduce the amount of ultraviolet energy reaching microorganisms. A reliable installation begins with water testing and pretreatment review, then continues with scheduled lamp, sleeve and filter service.

Important safety noteUV does not prove that water is safe, and a field observation cannot confirm bacteria. Positive results, boil-water notices or illness concerns require prompt action through an accredited laboratory and the appropriate health authority.

System sizing

Match validated flow capacity to the real peak demand rather than average household use.

Pretreatment review

Check sediment, hardness, iron, manganese and colour that can interfere with UV delivery.

Lamp and sleeve service

Replace lamps, clean or replace sleeves and confirm seals, sensors and alarms.

Installation review

Confirm bypass arrangement, shutoff protection, electrical supply and service access.

System sanitizing

Sanitize plumbing and treatment components when appropriate after installation or contamination.

Troubleshooting

Investigate alarms, low-intensity warnings, leaks, damaged sleeves and premature fouling.

How it works

Disinfection backed by testing and maintenance.

Equipment is selected only after the water, household demand, existing plumbing and maintenance expectations are understood.

  1. 1
    Use accredited testing

    A positive bacteria result should be confirmed and handled using appropriate laboratory and public-health guidance.

  2. 2
    Correct pretreatment issues

    Improve clarity and reduce fouling conditions before relying on UV.

  3. 3
    Size and install

    Match the certified system capacity to peak flow and provide safe service access.

  4. 4
    Maintain and retest

    Replace lamps on schedule, clean the sleeve and repeat microbiological testing as appropriate.
