Equipment service and maintenance

Water treatment service, repair and troubleshooting.

Existing equipment should be tested and understood before it is replaced. Many complaints come from settings, fouling, failed consumables, bypass conditions, leaks or treatment stages that no longer match the water for homes in Victoria, Langford and surrounding Greater Victoria communities.

What the assessment considers

Service can include

  • Water softeners and control valves
  • Iron, sulfur and sediment filters
  • UV lamps, sleeves, alarms and seals
  • RO filters, membranes and storage tanks
  • Chemical-feed pumps, tubing and contact systems

Practical system planning

Diagnose the system before selling another one.

A useful service visit compares raw and treated water, inspects the equipment sequence, checks programming and identifies maintenance that has been missed. The homeowner should understand whether the issue is repairable, a design limitation or a change in the water.

Performance checks

Compare raw and treated water and confirm whether each treatment stage is doing its job.

Softener troubleshooting

Investigate hardness breakthrough, brine problems, valve errors, leaks and programming.

Filter service

Change cartridges, inspect media filters, verify backwash and address pressure loss.

UV maintenance

Replace lamps, clean sleeves, correct alarms and inspect seals and pretreatment.

RO maintenance

Replace filters, sanitize, test membranes and correct low production or tank issues.

Chemical-feed service

Check pumps, tubing, injection valves, solution strength, contact time and residuals.

How it works

A clear service report and practical next step.

Equipment is selected only after the water, household demand, existing plumbing and maintenance expectations are understood.

  1. 1
    Listen to the complaint

    Document what changed, where it appears and whether the equipment ever worked properly.

  2. 2
    Test and inspect

    Compare raw and treated water and review the entire treatment sequence.

  3. 3
    Correct what is reasonable

    Service consumables, settings, leaks and obvious equipment faults when appropriate.

  4. 4
    Explain the outcome

    Separate confirmed findings, remaining uncertainty and the next recommended action.
