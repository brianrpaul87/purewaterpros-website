Drinking-water treatment

Reverse osmosis drinking water systems.

RO can provide a dedicated source of treated drinking and cooking water when the system, pretreatment, storage and maintenance plan fit the water for homes in Victoria, Langford and surrounding Greater Victoria communities.

What the assessment considers

RO performance depends on

  • Feed-water pressure and temperature
  • Dissolved-mineral load
  • Chlorine or chloramine exposure
  • Pretreatment and sediment control
  • Membrane, storage tank and faucet condition

Practical system planning

Good RO is about verified performance—not just filter changes.

A reverse-osmosis system needs adequate pressure, appropriate pretreatment, a healthy membrane and proper sanitizing. Performance should be checked with feed and product-water readings rather than judged by taste alone.

New RO systems

Under-sink systems selected around water quality, available pressure, space and household expectations.

Membrane testing

Compare feed and product-water readings to determine whether the membrane is still performing.

Filter service

Replace sediment and carbon stages at intervals appropriate for the water and usage.

Sanitizing

Clean and sanitize housings, tubing and storage components during appropriate service visits.

Low production

Investigate pressure, temperature, fouling, tank pressure, restrictors and blocked filters.

Existing equipment

Service and assess many common residential RO configurations, not only newly installed systems.

How it works

A drinking-water system that can be maintained.

Equipment is selected only after the water, household demand, existing plumbing and maintenance expectations are understood.

  1. 1
    Review the goal

    Clarify taste, dissolved-mineral or drinking-water concerns and what testing is available.

  2. 2
    Inspect the feed water

    Check pressure, pretreatment, chlorine exposure and potential membrane-fouling conditions.

  3. 3
    Install or restore

    Fit the system carefully, sanitize it and correct obvious performance restrictions.

  4. 4
    Verify performance

    Measure production and membrane performance and leave a clear filter schedule.
