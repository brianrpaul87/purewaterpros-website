Well Water Issues

Private wells vary seasonally and by geology. We test and size systems to your home’s water and flow. Below are common well‑water issues and proven solutions.

Hard Water (Calcium & Magnesium)

Symptoms: Hard water means your well or city water has too many calcium and magnesium minerals dissolved in it. These aren’t harmful to drink, but they cause: White scale on fixtures, kettles, and coffee makers Soap that won’t lather, leaving skin and hair dry Appliances (dishwashers, washing machines, water heaters) wearing out faster Higher energy bills because heating elements get coated with scale

Solutions: High‑efficiency water softener sized to hardness, iron, and peak flow.

Iron & Manganese

Symptoms: orange/brown (iron) or black/grey (manganese) staining; metallic taste.

Solutions: AIO (Air‑Injection Oxidation) filter with catalytic media; or oxidant injection (air/chlorine/peroxide) + media; often followed by softener.

Sulfur / “Rotten‑Egg” Odour (H₂S) Hydrogen Sulphide gas

Symptoms: strong odour at cold and/or hot taps; can vary by season.

Solutions: AIO sulfur filter (Air‑Injection Oxidation), (low–moderate H₂S) or chlorine injection + contact + catalytic carbon (moderate–high H₂S/bio‑sulfur). Check water‑heater anode if odour only in hot water.

Bacteria (Total Coliform / E. coli)

Risk: microbial contamination can cause illness. Any positive E. coli result is unsafe and requires action.

Continuous protection options: UV disinfection (no chemical taste) or continuous chlorination (with contact tank + carbon polish).

UV vs. Continuous Chlorination — How We Decide

Pick UV when…

  • After shock chlorination and fixes, repeat tests are negative (no E. coli / total coliform).
  • Raw water can meet UV pretreatment targets consistently:
  • • Turbidity low and water clear (good UV transmittance)
  • • Iron ≲ 0.3 mg/L and Manganese ≲ 0.05 mg/L (to avoid fouling)
  • • 5 µm sediment prefilter in place; hardness controlled near the UV

Pick Chlorination when…

  • Positives keep coming back despite shock + fixes.
  • You need a disinfectant residual in long plumbing or storage tanks.
  • Turbidity/iron/manganese/UVT vary and UV pretreatment can’t be kept in spec.
  • Then polish taste/odour with catalytic carbon after the contact tank.

Process: If a routine test comes back positive, boil + shock chlorinate, fix obvious intrusion, and retest (usually after ~10–14 days). If contamination recurs or pretreatment can’t hold specs, we install continuous chlorination. If stable and clear, UV is ideal.

Low pH (Acidic) / High pH

Symptoms: blue‑green stains (copper corrosion) with low pH; scaling with high pH.

Solutions: Calcite/corosex neutralizer for low pH; softener or scale control for high pH + hardness.

Sediment & Turbidity

Symptoms: cloudy water, clogged aerators/filters, pressure drop, seasonal changes.

Solutions: Properly sized sediment filtration (spin‑down, pleated, or backwashing turbidity units) before other equipment.

Tannins (Organics)

Tannins are natural organic matter that come from decaying leaves, roots, and other plant material as rain and groundwater move through soil and peat

Color: Yellow, brown, or “tea-stained” water, especially noticeable in sinks, tubs, and toilets.

Taste & Odor: Sometimes an earthy or musty flavor. Stains: Can discolor laundry (light clothes may look dingy) and leave fixtures looking stained even when they’re clean.

Solutions: Anion exchange for tannins; RO for drinking water; sometimes paired with carbon pretreatment.

Nitrates / Arsenic / Uranium (Site‑specific)

Nitrates are invisible contaminants that come from fertilizers, septic tanks, and soil runoff. You can’t see, smell, or taste them — the only way to know is by testing. They’re especially dangerous for infant, but the right filtration makes water safe again.

Solutions: Specialty media, anion exchange, or RO (point‑of‑use); lab testing determines the approach.
