Reverse Osmosis Drinking Water

Great-tasting, low-TDS water at your kitchen tap — perfect for drinking, coffee/tea, and crystal-clear ice.

What RO Does

Reverse osmosis pushes water through a semi-permeable membrane that rejects dissolved solids. With sediment and carbon stages, RO reduces TDS, sodium, chlorine/chloramine, many heavy metals, and emerging concerns like PFAS.

  • Clean, consistent taste for drinking & cooking
  • Great for coffee/espresso and clear ice
  • Helps protect kettles & small appliances from scale

How RO Works (Typical 4–5 Stage)

  • Stage 1 — Sediment (5 µm): Removes grit & rust to protect later stages.
  • Stage 2 — Carbon: Reduces chlorine/chloramine and organics before the membrane.
  • Stage 3 — RO Membrane: Rejects most dissolved solids (TDS, sodium, metals).
  • Stage 4 — Post-Carbon: Taste polish before the faucet.
  • Storage Tank & Faucet: On-demand flow for drinking & fridge/ice (optional).

When RO Makes the Biggest Difference

  • City water: Taste/odour from chlorine/chloramine; PFAS concerns; consistent low-TDS for beverages.
  • Well water: High sodium/TDS, nitrates, or other dissolved contaminants (use RO at the kitchen; pretreat if needed).
  • Coffee/Tea/Ice: Barista-level taste and clear cubes with stable mineral content.

What Is TDS & Why It Matters

TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) is the total concentration of dissolved minerals, salts, metals, and organic compounds in water. It’s measured in ppm (parts per million), which is essentially the same as mg/L for water.

Higher TDS doesn’t automatically mean unsafe, but it often means more mineral/salty taste and more scale in kettles and appliances. RO lowers TDS for better taste and less buildup.

💡 Note: ppm and mg/L are effectively interchangeable for TDS in water.

Alkaline / Remineralizing Filters

RO lowers dissolved minerals (TDS), which some people describe as a “flat” taste. A remineralizing stage adds a touch of calcium/magnesium and can gently raise pH.

  • What it is: A post-filter (often calcite or blended media) installed after the RO tank.
  • What it does: Adds small amounts of Ca/Mg for taste; typical pH ~7.5–8.5.
  • What it doesn’t do: It doesn’t convert water into high-TDS “alkaline” water; TDS stays low.

Choose remin if you prefer a rounder mouthfeel; skip it if you like ultra-crisp, low-TDS taste. Either way, you still get the core RO benefits.

RO FAQs

Does RO remove minerals — is that a problem?

Yes, RO lowers minerals; that’s what reduces TDS and scale. Most people get adequate minerals from food. If you prefer, add our remineralizing post-filter for taste.

Is RO wasteful?

Modern systems are efficient. Expect roughly ~2:1 to 3:1 concentrate:product ratios depending on pressure/temperature. We can optimize with a permeate pump.

Can RO feed my fridge/ice maker?

Yes — common setup. We’ll size the tank and run a line to your fridge for crystal-clear ice.

What’s the upkeep?

Prefilters every 6–12 months, post-carbon ~12 months, membrane 2–5 years depending on use and water quality.
