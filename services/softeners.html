Water Softeners

End hard water problems for good with high-efficiency softeners sized to your family and flow. Below you’ll find symptoms, sizing, options, and FAQs.

Why Soften?

Hard water has excess calcium & magnesium. It’s not a health risk, but it causes white scale, soap that won’t lather, and premature wear on water-using appliances.

  • Shinier fixtures and spot-free dishes
  • Softer-feeling skin & hair
  • Longer appliance life & lower energy use

Hard Water Symptoms

  • White crust on faucets and shower glass; showerheads spray unevenly
  • Scale in kettles & coffee makers — flaky bits or chalk rings
  • Soap won’t lather; skin and hair feel dry or dull
  • Stiff laundry and faded colours; more detergent needed
  • Higher energy bills as heating elements get coated with scale

What “Hardness” Measures

Hardness is the amount of calcium & magnesium in water, measured in grains per gallon (gpg) or mg/L (ppm as CaCO3). 1 gpg = 17.1 mg/L.

0–3SoftSoap lathers easily; minimal spotting

How We Size a Water Softener

We size based on your hardness, household use, and flow. The goal is a softener that regenerates about once a week, not every day.

Step-by-Step

  1. Measure hardness in grains per gallon (gpg).
  2. Estimate water use: people in home × ~60 gallons per person per day.
  3. Daily grains: Hardness (gpg) × Gallons/day.
  4. 7-day buffer: Daily grains × 7 = Weekly capacity.
  5. Choose the next size up so you’re not running on empty before regeneration.

Example Sizing

Hardness: 15 gpg · Household: 4 people

  • Water use ≈ 4 × 60 = 240 gal/day
  • Daily grains = 15 × 240 = 3,600 grains/day
  • Weekly capacity = 3,600 × 7 = 25,200 grains
  • Recommended size: 32,000-grain (1.0 cu ft)

Quick Guide by Household

  • 1–2 people, moderate hardness: 24k–32k (1.0 cu ft)
  • 3–4 people, average hardness: 40k–48k (1.5 cu ft)
  • 4–6 people, higher hardness: 64k (2.0 cu ft)
  • Larger homes or very hard water: 80k+ (custom sizing)

Softener FAQs

Will a softener lower my water pressure?

A correctly sized, maintained softener has minimal pressure drop. Undersized or clogged prefilters can cause noticeable drop — we size to your peak demand.

Do I need salt if I choose a softener?

Yes. Ion-exchange softeners regenerate with salt (sodium or potassium). If you prefer no salt, ask about TAC conditioning systems.

What if I also have iron, manganese, or tannins?

We stage treatment so the softener is protected with pre-filtration. This prevents resin fouling and keeps your system efficient.
