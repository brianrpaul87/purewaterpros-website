Iron & Sulfur Filters

Remove orange/brown staining and rotten-egg odour for good. We test, size, and install systems that actually solve the problem.

What’s causing it?

Iron (Fe) occurs naturally in many wells and leaves orange/brown staining on fixtures, tubs, laundry, and dishwashers. A metallic taste is common.

Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) is a gas produced by sulfur-reducing bacteria or chemical reactions in aquifers. It creates the familiar “rotten-egg” smell, often stronger on hot water as gas is released faster.

Both issues are mainly nuisance/maintenance problems, but they’re frustrating and can damage appliances and finishes over time.

Rotten-egg smell Hydrogen sulfide gas Hot water taps/showers, sometimes cold
Metallic taste Iron &/or manganese Drinking water, ice, coffee/tea

The Right Fix (Sized to Your Water)

  • Air Injection / Catalytic Media (AIO): Introduces air to oxidize iron; catalytic media captures & backwashes. Great for many Fe problems.
  • Ozone Injection: Powerful oxidizer for stubborn iron/H2S; very effective and chemical-free at the tap.
  • Chlorination + Contact Tank + Carbon: Metered dosing for heavy H2S or iron bacteria. Carbon polishes taste/odour after oxidation.
  • Dedicated H2S Filters: Specialty media designed for sulfur gas removal.
  • Softener (as a partner): Protects fixtures and tackles hardness. We keep iron OUT of the softener whenever levels are high.

We choose the method after a proper test: iron/manganese levels, presence of sulfur, flow rate, and plumbing layout all matter.

FAQs

Is iron or sulfur harmful?

Usually they’re nuisance issues. We still recommend treatment to protect fixtures, appliances, and quality of life.

Why is the smell worse on hot water?

Heating releases dissolved hydrogen sulfide gas faster, so odour is more noticeable at showers and hot taps.

Will a softener remove iron?

Small traces may be captured, but a softener is not the primary iron filter. We oxidize and filter iron first, then soften for hardness.

Do I need chemicals?

Not always. Many homes are solved with air or ozone. For heavy sulfur or iron bacteria, metered chlorination with carbon polishing is best.
