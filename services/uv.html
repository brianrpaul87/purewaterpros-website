UV Disinfection Systems

Protect your home from bacteria without chemicals. UV neutralizes microbes (bacteria, viruses, cysts) as water flows past the lamp.

How UV Keeps Water Safe

Ultraviolet light damages microbial DNA/RNA so organisms can’t reproduce. It’s a fast, point-of-entry treatment that doesn’t change taste, odour, or pH.

  • Chemical-free disinfection at every tap
  • Effective against total coliform, E. coli, and many viruses/cysts
  • Low maintenance: annual lamp change, sleeve cleaning

Pretreatment Checklist (for reliable UV)

  • Sediment: 5 µm prefilter keeps the quartz sleeve clear.
  • Iron: ≤ 0.3 ppm (mg/L) recommended before UV.
  • Manganese: ≤ 0.05 ppm (mg/L) recommended before UV.
  • Turbidity: Clear water (ideally < ~1 NTU) so light penetrates fully.
  • Hardness: If > ~7 gpg (~120 ppm as CaCO3 / mg/L), add softening to reduce scaling on the sleeve.
  • Chlorine/Chloramine present? Add a carbon stage before UV to protect taste and help keep the sleeve film-free.

We test on site and size pretreatment so your UV runs at peak dose continuously.

UV vs. Chlorine — When is Chlorine Needed?

UV is typically enough when…

  • Source is microbiologically unsafe (e.g., well with coliform/E. coli) but water is clear and within pretreatment limits: Fe ≤ 0.3 ppm (mg/L), Mn ≤ 0.05 ppm (mg/L), turbidity low.
  • You want chemical-free disinfection at point-of-entry.

Chlorine is recommended when…

  • There’s a need for a residual disinfectant in storage/distribution (cisterns, long plumbing runs, small community/park systems).
  • You’re dealing with iron bacteria/biofilm or a contamination event—perform shock chlorination then maintain UV.
  • Fe or Mn routinely exceed pretreatment limits and cause sleeve fouling; chlorination + contact time + carbon may be the robust approach.

Many homes use both approaches strategically: chlorine for residual or heavy problems, carbon to polish, and UV as a final barrier.

UV FAQs

Does UV change taste or add chemicals?

No—UV doesn’t add anything to the water and doesn’t change taste or odour.

Will UV remove chlorine or chloramine?

No—use an activated carbon stage to reduce chlorine/chloramine ahead of UV (and improve taste).

What maintenance is required?

Change the UV lamp annually, clean the quartz sleeve, and replace sediment/carbon filters as scheduled.

My well had a positive coliform test—what should I do?

Perform shock chlorination of the well/plumbing, flush, verify a clean follow-up test, then rely on the UV barrier day-to-day.
